Ph.D. Candidate in Economics
Concordia University

I am a Ph.D. student in economics at Concordia University. My research interests are Labor Economics, Economics of Education and Human Capital, and Applied Econometrics.

Currently, I am conducting empirical research using an American survey. My Ph.D. research investigates the technology of cognitive and noncognitive skill formation in young teenagers. Specifically, my research utilizes data from the National Education Longitudinal Survey of 1988 (NELS:88) and will focus on estimating the process of young teenagers' (14-18 years old) cognitive and noncognitive skill formation using a dynamic latent factor structure. I am using the identification method presented by Agostinelli and Wiswall (2020) with the constraint on the production function and their sequential estimation algorithm for the joint dynamic process of latent investment and skill development.
